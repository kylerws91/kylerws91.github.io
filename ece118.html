    • Mechatronics Course Final Project

    A summary of a robot developed from scratch under competition constraints.

    Objective and Setup

    This project details a robot competition between students of the mechatronics course, completed in a single month timeframe. In a 8' x 8' course, three triangular towers will be placed in random position and orientation. The overarching goal is to navigate to the towers and score in a designated area as fast as possible. Each tower will emit a 2 kHz signal to be picked up upon. Each tower will also feature one side layered with a track wire with a 22 kHz signal running through it. Each side will have three holes with a visual indicator on where to shoot the ball. Beyond the towers, the obstacle course features black tape borders, several interefence signals to be filtered as well as blocks. The robot I designed along with a team, managed to score in all towers under two minutes and will be discussed further below.

    Mechanical Design and Layout

    The mechanical design phase initiated with an in-depth collaborative discussion and brainstorming of individual concepts. Our final design consistented of three layers of MDF laser cut to minimize weight and complexity. Using SolidWorks, we laid out our sensors that made the most intuitive sense. We had four bumper sensors next to our motors on the first layer with some supports 1/8'' off the ground. The second layer contains a micro-controller, a simple tank circuit and a color sensor. The top layer contains a beacon detector circuit, two tape sensor circuits as well as the launching mechanism. Each piece is laser cut and fabricated by hand./

    Electrical Engineering

    This component involved the meticulous assembly and integration of all electrical elements. From designing analog filters to pick up specific signals, to configuring power distribution boards for appropriate voltage ratings, every part was crafted with precision. This ensured the effective and efficient functioning of the robot, highlighting the project's innovative engineering prowess.

    Software Development

    The heart of our robot's functionality lies in its software, encompassing programming for motors, infrared sensors, servos, and bumper mechanisms. An intelligent state machine algorithm forms the behavioral backbone of the robot, enabling it to interact with its environment in a meaningful and goal-oriented manner. The implementation of various states and behaviors presented challenges but ultimately led to a responsive and capable autonomous robot.

    Conclusion

    The project concluded with the successful integration of the three-layer configuration for our robot, addressing challenges like wheel alignment and signal interference. The adjustments and redesigns significantly enhanced our robot's navigation and alignment capabilities, demonstrating the project's success in fostering a deeper understanding and appreciation of the multifaceted field of mechatronics.
